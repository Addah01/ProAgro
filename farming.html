POST HARVEST SCIENCE

The term postharvest science denotes a dynamic interconnected functions or operations within a particular sphere of activity from harvesting up to the time the product reach the ultimate consumer. The process look at as a chain of activities from pre harvest through a a conduit of products shelf life by which they undergo a functional succession of various operations.

MAJOR FOOD PRODUCTS

The following classification of food crops was developed by NSPRI to effectively conduct its services.
A.Durables – those are called grain or field crops and are stored at safe moisture content (SMC), and can be stored for a long period of time with minimum deterioration except otherwise attack by insects and rodents e.g.
i) Cereals – Corn, sorghum, millet, rice, wheat, e.t.c.
ii) Legumes – Pigeon pea, cowpea, soybean, bambaranut, e.t.c.
iii. Oil seeds- melon, peanuts, sun flower, Sesame, e.t.c
B. Semi-Perishables – those are agricultural commodities that can be stored shortly. Their shelf life can be increased when they are stored under high humidity but can be highly susceptible to pathogenic attacks from fungal and rodents.
i) Roots – cassava, e.t.c.
ii) Tubers–Yam, taro e.t.c.
C. Perishables – those are agricultural produce that are highly perishable with a natural shelf life of a few days because of their inherent moisture content. The pre disposing factors to perishability of those crops are physiological (temperature, moisture, time) pathological (fungal, bacteria, virus) and entomological (insects).
a) Fruits – Carrots, Aubergeen, tomatoes e.t.c.
b) Vegetables – Lettuce, Amaranth, Cauliflower e.t.c.
D. Other agricultural Products: these are agricultural products of plants and origin. They can be easily spoilt by physical and chemical properties of the products. Spoilage results in foul odors, bubbles, putrid, moulds and color change. Microorganisms and insects are the major factors of spoilage.
a) Fish
b) Meat
c) Milk
